THE GEOMETRY

The court is a strict mathematical grid. A standard tennis court measures 78 feet (23.77 meters) in length.The width is 27 feet (8.23 meters) for singles matches and expands to 36 feet (10.97 meters) for doubles.

02

STROKES

Service

The overhead starting shot that uses height and power to launch the ball into the opponent's service box.

Forehand

A dominant swing from your natural side, designed to dictate the game with speed and heavy topspin.

Backhand

A stroke on the opposite side using one hand for reach or two hands for more control and stability.

Volley

A quick, short punch hit before the ball bounces, used at the net to finish the point instantly.

03

THE ABSOLUTE LAWS

Tennis is governed by strict parameters that dictate the flow of the game.

I. The Serve & Faults

The server must hit the ball into the diagonally opposite service box without touching the net or stepping over the baseline. Two failed attempts result in a double fault, gifting the point to the opponent.

II. The Tie-Break

A special game played at 6-6 in a set to decide the winner, where the first player to reach 7 points with a 2-point lead wins.

III. The Hawk-Eye

A high-tech tracking system that uses multiple cameras to determine with millimeter precision if a ball is "in" or "out. If the ball touches even a millimeter of the boundary line, it is considered "In" but it must be hit back before it bounces a second time on the ground.

IV. Net Touch Rule

If a player, their racket, or their clothing touches the net or its posts while the ball is still in play, they immediately lose the point.

THE BATTLEGROUNDS

The court is a strict mathematical grid of 23.77m by 8.23m. But the surface changes the entire physics of the game.

Red Clay

High bounce, slow pace. The ultimate test of endurance.

Grass

Low, skidding bounce. Favors aggressive, lightning-fast play.

Hard Court

The perfect equilibrium of speed and true bounce.
